Ayurvedic Vegetarian Guatemalan Cuisine

Every meal honors local tradition and Ayurvedic balance (sattvic, seasonal, easy to digest). All dishes are 100% vegetarian, with vegan and gluten-free options.

Cooking Class Recipes

Pepián de Calabaza: A rich, nutty stew made with roasted squash, tomatoes, sesame, pumpkin seeds, and warm spices—simmered for hours.

Tamales Colorados: Masa filled with vegetables, wrapped in banana leaves, steamed until fragrant.

Jocón de Hongos: Green stew with tomatillo, cilantro, and mushrooms—light yet deeply satisfying.

Atol de Elote: Sweet corn drink, creamy and comforting—perfect for cool evenings.

Practical Guide: What to Bring

🧳 Clothing

Yoga/Morning: Light, breathable layers (leggings, tank, light sweater for cool mornings)

Day Tours: Comfortable walking sandals, sun hat, light rain jacket (brief afternoon showers)

Evenings: Casual dress or nice top for farewell dinner (no heels needed)

Swim/Spa: Swimsuit, cover-up, flip-flops

💄 Cosmetics & Jewelry

Minimal makeup (natural light is kind!). Bring favorite earrings or a meaningful necklace—great for photos. Sunscreen (reef-safe), insect repellent, lip balm, moisturizer.

💰 Money & Essentials

Currency: Guatemalan Quetzal (GTQ). Rate: 1 CAD ≈ 5.8 GTQ.

Spending: $20–40 CAD/day for souvenirs, tips, extras.

Bring: Reusable water bottle, journal, pen, small backpack, power adapter (Type A/B), travel insurance documents.
