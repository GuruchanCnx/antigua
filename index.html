Your Home: Casa San Juan

Nestled in the historic village of San Juan del Obispo—home to the 17th-century palace of Francisco Marroquín, the first bishop of the Americas—Casa San Juan is your exclusive retreat sanctuary just 15 minutes from Antigua, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Behind vine-covered walls, discover a lush garden, an architecturally enchanting main house, and six unique, modern rooms and lofts. The friendly staff attends to your every need, ensuring comfort, safety, and serenity throughout your stay.

As a guesthouse and retreat center specializing in yoga and Ayurveda, Casa San Juan offers the perfect balance of tranquility and cultural access—your true home base in Guatemala.

About Jean Amabile

J

Jean Amabile is a 73-year-old Canadian wellness guide, lifelong knitter, and passionate advocate for vibrant aging. After decades of meaningful work and family life, Jean now creates intimate retreats where women can reconnect—with themselves, each other, and the wisdom of ancient cultures.

A daily yogi and lover of nourishing, high-protein meals, Jean has led small-group journeys to Guatemala since 2023. Her retreats are known for their authenticity, attention to dietary needs, and deep respect for Mayan traditions.

“At this stage of life,” Jean says, “we don’t need more stuff. We need meaningful moments—shared over a handmade tortilla, a sunrise yoga session, or a quiet walk through a Mayan village.”

Your 7-Night Itinerary: June 27 – July 5, 2026

Saturday, June 27 – Arrival & Sacred Welcome

Private transfer from La Aurora International Airport to Casa San Juan (45 minutes). Settle into your room with a welcome drink of hibiscus tea. At 6:00 PM, join Jean in the garden for a gentle opening circle: share your name, your intention, and receive a Mayan blessing. Enjoy a light dinner of caldo de res, grilled plantains, and handmade tortillas.

Sunday, June 28 – Market, Fire & Flavor

6:45 AM: Sunrise yoga on the garden terrace. Breakfast with tropical fruits and local coffee. Travel to Antigua for a guided market immersion. Then, prepare Cooking Class #1: authentic pepián and tamales colorados. Share your meal, then reflect in the evening circle.

Monday, June 29 – Threads & Chocolate

Morning yoga. Journey to Lake Atitlán and San Juan La Laguna. Visit a women-led textile cooperative. After lunch, join a hands-on bean-to-bar chocolate workshop. Return to Casa San Juan for dinner.

Tuesday, June 30 – Antigua’s Soul

Meditation and gentle yoga. Explore Antigua with a local historian. After lunch, create Cooking Class #2: jocón and plátanos en mole. Evening free for rest or exploration.

Wednesday, July 1 – Highland Hands

Morning yoga. Visit San Antonio Palopó for black pottery. Then, prepare Cooking Class #3: fiambre and atol de elote. Afternoon free—opt for a garden massage or relax in the courtyard.

Thursday, July 2 – Iximche: Where Time Bends

Early departure. At Iximche—capital of the Kaqchikel Maya—walk ancient plazas. At noon, participate in an optional Mayan Fire Ceremony. Lunch with mountain views. Return by 5:00 PM.

Friday, July 3 – Give & Receive

Final yoga session. Spend the morning volunteering at a local school. After lunch, visit the artisan market. That evening, celebrate at Mesón Panza Verde with a multi-course farewell dinner. Close the circle with tears, laughter, and promises.

Saturday, July 4 – Gentle Departures

Optional sunrise yoga. Share a final breakfast together. From 9:00 AM onward, enjoy private airport transfers based on your flight schedule.
