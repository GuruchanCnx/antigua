Introduction

The Wisdom of Seasons Within Us

Dear Sister,

At 73 years old, I've walked through many seasons of life—motherhood, career, empty nesting, and now, this beautiful chapter of elderhood. What I've learned is this: life's transitions are not problems to solve, but sacred thresholds to cross.

Just as Guatemala's rainy season brings both abundance and challenges to Casa San Juan's garden, our inner landscapes transform too. Abhyanga

Ritual #1

Morning Abhyanga (Self-Massage with Oil)

Why It Matters

When life feels uncertain, our nervous system craves grounding touch. Abhyanga—warm oil massage—calms Vata (the Ayurvedic principle of movement and change) and creates a protective boundary around your energy field.

When to Practice
  • Upon waking, before breakfast
  • During times of anxiety or decision-making
  • After travel or major life changes
The Simple Practice

1. Choose your oil: Sesame oil for cool/dry seasons, coconut for warm/humid days.

2. Warm the oil: Place a small amount in a bowl and warm it slightly.

3. Massage sequence:
- Scalp (2 mins): circular motions
- Face/Ears (1 min): gentle strokes
- Arms/Hands (2 mins): strokes to heart
- Abdomen (1 min): clockwise circles
- Legs/Feet (3 mins): upward strokes

4. Rest: Sit quietly for 5 minutes before showering.

My Personal Reflection
"When I retired at 55, I felt untethered—like a tree without roots. Every morning at 5:30 AM, I began with abhyanga... The oil became a boundary between me and the world's demands."

Agni

Ritual #2

Sipping Warm Water with Lemon

Why It Matters

In Ayurveda, digestion (agni) is the foundation of all health. During transitions, our digestive fire fluctuates, leading to fatigue and brain fog. Warm lemon water reignites agni gently.

Ayurvedic Wisdom

The lemon's sour taste stimulates enzymes, while warm water flushes toxins (ama). This is vital during "rainy seasons" of life when we hold stagnation.

When to Practice
  • Every morning (non-negotiable)
  • Before difficult conversations
The Simple Practice

1. The night before: Fill a glass with room-temp water and a lemon slice.

2. Upon waking: Drink at room temperature (not cold).

3. Breathe: Take three deep breaths before your first sip.

4. Wait: Allow 15-20 minutes before eating or drinking anything else.

My Personal Reflection
"Ginger explained, 'It's not the water. It's the pause between waking and doing. That's where clarity lives.' Six months later, a participant told me this was the one practice she continued."

Journal

Ritual #3

Evening Journaling with Moon Questions

Why It Matters

Nighttime is ruled by Vata, making sleep restless during life changes. Journaling creates a container for swirling thoughts, allowing them to be witnessed rather than carried into dreams.

The Simple Practice

1. Set space 15 mins before bed. Light a candle.

2. Ask ONE question:

  • Mon: What am I releasing?
  • Tue: Where did I feel alive?
  • Wed: What does my body need?
  • Thu: Who blessed me?
  • Fri: What truth must I speak?
  • Sat: What beauty did I see?
  • Sun: What am I leaving behind?
Process

Write freely: No editing, no judging—just let the pen move.

Close with: A hand over your heart, whispering "Thank you".

My Personal Reflection
"After my husband separated, I couldn't sleep. One night I wrote: 'What am I releasing?' The answer came: 'The need to have said everything.' That simple question created a portal through grief."

Breathe

Ritual #4

Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril Breathing)

Why It Matters

When life shifts, breath becomes shallow. Nadi Shodhana ("channel cleaning") balances the left (moon/cooling) and right (sun/heating) hemispheres of the brain, restoring harmony without forcing change.

When to Practice
  • Upon waking
  • Before important decisions
  • Moments of overwhelm
The Simple Practice

1. Sit comfortably: Spine straight.

2. Hand position: Right hand in Vishnu mudra (index/middle fingers tucked).

3. Sequence:
- Close right, Inhale left (4)
- Close both (2)
- Exhale right (6), Inhale right (4)
- Close both (2)
- Exhale left (6)

4. Repeat: 5 cycles.

My Personal Reflection
"Ginger guided me through this... 'Your breath is always home,' she reminded me. Today, I teach this to every woman—watching shoulders drop and eyes brighten."

Nourish

Ritual #5

Sattvic Evening Meal

Why It Matters

Food is medicine. During transitions, we need sattvic (pure) foods—nourishing but light enough to support restful sleep and clear thinking.

3 Sattvic Staples

1. Kitchari: Mung beans & rice with spices.
2. Steamed greens: With lemon and ghee.
3. Warm spiced milk: With cardamom/nutmeg.

The Simple Practice

1. Timing: Eat by 7 PM.

2. Portion: 1/2 Veg, 1/4 Grain, 1/4 Protein.

3. Mindfulness: No screens, no rush. Cook with love.

My Personal Reflection
"During my separation, I ate takeout in front of the TV. My sleep vanished. When I began cooking simple meals, the act became a promise: 'You are worth nourishing.'"
